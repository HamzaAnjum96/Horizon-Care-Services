# Horizon Care Services Website Copy Workshop (Simulated)

Date: 2026-05-19  
Scope: Whole public site except blog content  
Purpose: Record an end-to-end working discussion between leadership, delivery, web, PM, and care-business focus group participants to inform a deeper rewrite.

- **PM (Website/Product PM)**

## 1) Opening context

**Facilitator:** We’re here because the current site explains *what* you do, but still under-explains *how* you work and *why your approach reduces risk for clients*. This meeting is to pressure-test positioning, objections, proof style, structure, and conversion paths.

**Owner 2 (Commercial):** We need copy that sounds like real operations, not generic agency language. Buyers should quickly understand: if they call us, what happens next, what standard of communication they can expect, and whether we’re realistic about capacity.

**Focus Group A (Care Home Manager):** My main question on agency sites is always: “Will this become more admin for us?” If yes, I leave.

**Focus Group B (Local Authority):** For us it’s speed + judgement. We need to know the agency won’t just throw names at us.

**Focus Group C (Supported Living):** Fit matters heavily. A “technically available” person can still be wrong for the setting.

---

## 2) Business reality: what Horizon actually wants to be known for

### Roundtable

**Owner 1 (Operations):** We want to be known for being usable under pressure. When a service is short, we stay clear and practical.

**Staffing Coordinator:** The first interaction is where confidence is won or lost. We ask precise questions quickly: role, setting, urgency, reporting expectations, practical constraints.

**Quality & Service Lead:** “How we work” is our differentiator. We do not treat every setting as interchangeable. That should be prominent everywhere.

**Recruitment Lead:** Candidate-side promise must align with client-side reality. If we attract people who only want easy shifts with no accountability, client experience drops.

**PM:** So the narrative should be: **setting-aware matching + clear communication + honest capacity + continuity after placement**.

### Agreed positioning statement (working)

Horizon is a healthcare staffing partner for organisations that need dependable cover under real-world pressure, with a process built around setting-fit, straight communication, and accountable follow-through.

## 3) Audience segmentation and conversion intent

### Segment 1: Organisations needing staff (primary revenue)

**Who:** NHS teams, local authority services, care homes, supported living, broader care organisations.  
**Moment:** Operational pressure (absence, rota gaps, service expansion, discharge pressure, recurring gaps).  
**Primary action:** Submit staffing requirement / call for urgent support.  
**Decision criteria:** responsiveness, realism, fit judgement, ease of process, communication quality.

### Segment 2: Professionals joining register (supply quality)

**Who:** Nurses, social workers, OTs, physios, HCAs, support workers.  
**Moment:** Looking for stable agency relationship with usable support.  
**Primary action:** Apply / join register.  
**Decision criteria:** quality of placements, respect, clarity, coordinator accessibility, reliability of process.

### Segment 3: Passive validators (procurement/compliance-aware stakeholders)

**Who:** People who review providers after first internal recommendation.  
**Moment:** “Can we trust this partner operationally?”  
**Primary action:** Validate credibility signals and process clarity.  
**Decision criteria:** specificity, consistency, absence of inflated claims, practical working method.

## 4) Biggest copy problems found on current site

### Panel comments

**Focus Group A:** Too many agencies say “high quality care.” I need to see *what they actually do* that lowers failure risk.

**Focus Group B:** I need a clear “if you contact us now, this is the exact process in 4 steps.”

**Focus Group C:** I want to see that the agency understands environment differences, not just role titles.

**Web Developer:** From UX perspective, we need section hierarchy where scanning answers 5 questions fast:
1. Do you handle my type of requirement?
2. How do you decide suitability?
3. What do you need from me to start?
4. How quickly will I hear back?
5. What happens if requirement changes?

**PM:** Current copy has improved tone but still lacks depth on operating model and collaboration behaviors.

---

## 5) Operating model to translate into copy (no certifications/regulatory claims)

### “How we work” framework (approved for future copy)

1. **Clarify the requirement**  
   We collect practical details quickly and remove ambiguity early.

2. **Assess suitability by setting**  
   We evaluate candidate fit against service context, not only job title.

3. **Confirm availability honestly**  
   We give clear yes/no and realistic timing.

4. **Prepare a clean handover**  
   We confirm logistics and expectations so placements start smoothly.

5. **Stay engaged after start**  
   We remain reachable for adjustments, extensions, and issue handling.

### Cultural principles (approved language direction)

- **Clarity over spin**
- **Accountability over volume**
- **Fit over speed-only matching**
- **Partnership over transaction**
- **Straight answers, especially under pressure**

## 6) Objections and how copy should answer them

### Objection map

**Objection:** “You’ll send whoever is free.”  
**Answer direction:** Explain setting-led matching process and what inputs are checked before proposing cover.

**Objection:** “We won’t know where things stand.”  
**Answer direction:** Promise concrete communication cadence (initial response + updates + change handling).

**Objection:** “We’ll have to repeat details constantly.”  
**Answer direction:** Emphasize structured intake and named coordination.

**Objection:** “You disappear after the shift is filled.”  
**Answer direction:** State after-placement ownership and escalation support.

**Objection:** “You’ll overpromise to win the booking.”  
**Answer direction:** Reinforce honest capacity and explicit no-when-not-safe stance.

## 7) Page-by-page direction

## Homepage

**Goal:** Fast confidence + immediate routing to staffing request.  
**Must convey:** who it’s for, operating approach, how to start now.  
**Additions needed:**
- Short “How we work in practice” strip (3–5 concise commitments)
- Micro-section: “What to send us for faster matching”
- Clear reassurance on change handling if needs evolve

## Services page

**Goal:** Replace generic role list feel with practical use-case utility.  
**Additions needed:**
- Requirement types by scenario (urgent cover, planned, recurring)
- “How we decide fit” explainer
- “What happens after you send a request” mini-flow

## About page

**Goal:** Explain business philosophy as operational method, not brand story fluff.  
**Additions needed:**
- Explicit working principles with real-world implications
- “How we work with clients day-to-day” behaviors
- “How we work with professionals” expectations and support model

## Work for us page

**Goal:** Attract candidates who match client expectations and process discipline.  
**Additions needed:**
- “What good looks like in placements”
- “What you can expect from us” and “what we expect from you”
- Better pre-qualification cues to improve applicant fit

## Contact page

**Goal:** Reduce friction and improve first-message quality.  
**Additions needed:**
- Recommended request template fields
- Explicit urgent vs non-urgent routes
- Clarify how quickly each route gets first response

---

## 8) Voice and tone guardrails for next rewrite

- Professional, calm, direct
- Specific wording over slogans
- No inflated outcomes or unverifiable claims
- No references to certifications, awards, or regulatory status unless explicitly provided and verified
- Prefer practical verbs: clarify, confirm, match, update, coordinate, resolve

---

## 9) Content architecture proposal (site-wide)

1. **Primary promise** (one clear line)  
2. **Who we support** (organisation types)  
3. **How we work** (process with plain steps)  
4. **What we place / where** (roles + settings)  
5. **When to call us** (situations and triggers)  
6. **What to send us** (intake checklist)  
7. **How we communicate** (response + updates + changes)  
8. **CTA by audience** (request staff / join register)

---

## 10) Focus group verbatims to preserve in future copy

> “Don’t tell me you care. Tell me what happens after I call.”

> “If you can’t do it, say that fast. That builds trust.”

> “Show me you understand that different settings need different people.”

> “I want less sales language and more operating language.”

---

## 11) Decisions made

- Next pass should be a **substantial rewrite**, not line edits.
- Copy should foreground **process, culture, and ways of working**.
- Keep claims practical and non-regulatory.
- Build page sections that reduce buyer effort and uncertainty.
- Use organisation-facing conversion path as primary, candidate path secondary but aligned.

## 13) Notes on constraints from this workshop

- No claims about certifications, compliance badges, or formal regulatory assurances.
- No invented statistics, testimonials, awards, or guarantees.
- Emphasize process quality and communication standards that are within operational control.
