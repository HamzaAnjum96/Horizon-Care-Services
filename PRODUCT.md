# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three distinct groups arrive at this site with different needs and different trust thresholds:

**Commissioning teams and local authorities (B2B):** Procurement-minded, risk-averse. They need to feel that Horizon is operationally credible and professionally accountable before they'll consider a referral or placement. They're scanning for evidence of structure, coverage, and competence.

**Families and individuals seeking supported accommodation (B2C):** Emotionally loaded decision. They're looking for safety and reassurance — that their person will be genuinely cared for, not processed. They need to feel taken care of themselves before they can trust Horizon with someone they love.

**Healthcare professionals seeking staffing work (B2E):** Practical and selective. They want a credible employer with real opportunities, fair treatment, and operational clarity. They're evaluating whether Horizon is worth their time.

## Product Purpose

Horizon Care Services Ltd is a UK health and social care provider operating across Bedfordshire, Buckinghamshire, Cambridgeshire, and Hertfordshire. It provides supported accommodation for adults with mental health needs and learning disabilities, and vetted staffing solutions for care providers needing urgent cover or planned placements.

The website exists to establish trust with all three user groups and convert that trust into contact: a referral enquiry, a placement request, or a job application. Success means a user confident enough to pick up the phone or submit the form.

## Brand Personality

Professional, warm, human.

The brand should read like a person you'd trust with something important — not a corporation, not a charity, not a clinical service. Unhurried and assured. It doesn't try too hard.

For commissioners: authority through specificity, not volume.
For families: care that feels genuine, not performed.
For candidates: a real employer with real standards.

## Anti-references

- Purple/teal "AI-generated healthcare" aesthetic — aurora effects, glowing gradients, neon accents. The previous version of this site. Explicitly rejected.
- Generic NHS-adjacent clinical white — cold, impersonal, interchangeable with any health provider.
- Charity-sector over-warmth — stock photography of smiling faces, platitudes about "making a difference", visual softness that undermines professional credibility.
- SaaS dashboard masquerading as care — flat icons, stat grids, hero metrics. This is a human service, not a product.

## Design Principles

**Quiet confidence.** Authority through restraint, not spectacle. The design earns trust by not demanding attention.

**Three audiences, one voice.** Professional enough for commissioners, warm enough for families, credible enough for candidates — achieved through the same visual language, not by switching registers.

**Show, don't reassure.** Specific details (bed spaces, counties, roles, stats) over generic claims ("caring team", "committed to quality"). Concrete evidence, not adjectives.

**Trust before transaction.** Every page earns the next step before asking for action. No aggressive CTAs until the user has been given a reason.

**Person-centred at the surface.** Copy and layout treat users as whole people. No reducing users to categories or care to a transaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA target. Screen reader compatibility is a priority: semantic HTML, meaningful alt text, logical heading hierarchy, focusable interactive elements with visible focus states. No specific reduced-motion requirement, but honour `prefers-reduced-motion` for scroll animations and transitions as good practice.
