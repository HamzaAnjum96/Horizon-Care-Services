We may collect and process the following categories of personal information:

Name, email address, phone number, postal address, and the content of any messages sent through our website, by email, or by post.

CV/résumé, employment history, qualifications, professional registrations, references, right-to-work documentation, DBS check results, training records, and any other information submitted as part of an application or employment relationship.

Name, contact details, care and support needs, health and medical information, next-of-kin details, and any information provided by referrers, social workers, or healthcare professionals for the purpose of arranging or delivering care.

Anonymised or aggregated usage data collected via essential cookies (see Section 9).

3. How We Use Your Information

We use personal information to:

  • Respond to enquiries and provide information about our services
  • Process and manage job applications and employment
  • Carry out DBS checks and reference verifications
  • Deliver supported accommodation and staffing services
  • Comply with legal, regulatory, and safeguarding obligations
  • Contact next of kin or emergency contacts when necessary
  • Maintain records required by law or professional standards
  • Improve our services and website

4. Legal Bases for Processing

We rely on the following lawful bases under UK GDPR Article 6:

  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): Responding to enquiries, managing our business operations, and improving our services.
  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to fulfil or prepare a contract — including staffing placements, service agreements, and employment contracts.
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): Complying with CQC regulations, employment law, safeguarding duties, and other applicable legislation.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): Where you have given explicit consent, such as subscribing to communications. You may withdraw consent at any time.
  • Vital interests (Art. 6(1)(d)): In emergency situations where processing is necessary to protect someone's life.

Special Category Data

Health and care-related information is processed under Article 9(2)(h) (healthcare and social care purposes) and Article 9(2)(b) (employment and social security) where applicable, always with appropriate safeguards in place.

5. How We Share Your Information

We may share personal information with:

  • Healthcare professionals, NHS trusts, local authorities, and partner organisations directly involved in delivering care or staffing services
  • Regulatory bodies such as the Care Quality Commission (CQC) where required by law
  • Referrers and support networks involved in an individual's care plan, with appropriate consent
  • Third-party service providers who process data on our behalf (e.g. IT support), under strict data processing agreements
  • Law enforcement or safeguarding agencies where required or permitted by law

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal information with third parties for marketing or any commercial purpose.

6. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as is necessary for the purpose it was collected, or as required by law:

Data Type Retention Period
General enquiry communications 2 years
Unsuccessful job applications 6 months
Employee records 7 years after employment ends
Service user / care records 8 years after service ends (or as required by CQC)
Referral and support plan records 8 years

After these periods, data is securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with our data retention procedures.

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (Subject Access Request)
  • Right to rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — request deletion of your data where there is no lawful basis for continued processing
  • Right to restriction — ask us to restrict processing in certain circumstances
  • Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at admin@horizon-careservices.co.uk, marking your message "Data Protection Request". We will respond within one calendar month. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

8. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal information against unauthorised access, accidental loss, disclosure, or destruction. Staff with access to personal data are trained in data protection obligations and bound by confidentiality agreements.

Where personal data is shared with third parties, we ensure suitable contractual safeguards are in place.

9. Cookies

Our website uses only essential cookies necessary for basic functionality. We do not use tracking, advertising, or analytics cookies without your explicit consent. You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings at any time; however, doing so may affect the functionality of the website.

10. Children

Our website is not directed at children under the age of 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children without appropriate parental or guardian consent. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data relating to a child, please contact us immediately at admin@horizon-careservices.co.uk and we will take prompt action.

11. International Transfers

All personal data we process is stored and handled within the United Kingdom. We do not transfer personal data outside the UK. Should this change, we will update this policy and ensure appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with UK GDPR requirements.
