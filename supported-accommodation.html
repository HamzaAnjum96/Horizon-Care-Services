Supported Accommodation

High-quality homes with tailored care and support.

Our schemes provide a safe and supportive living environment for people with mental health needs and learning disabilities, helping residents build confidence, life skills, and long-term independence.

Type of schemes How we support Accommodation projects

Type of schemes

Our support is flexible and based on individual needs, from a few hours weekly to intensive daily interventions that support recovery goals.

  • Adult Placement Scheme: Longer placements, usually 6 months to 2 years, with transition planning toward independent living.
  • Short-Term Support: Focused support over 1 to 6 months to stabilize wellbeing and build resilience.
  • Step-Down Transitional Support: Short intensive support after hospital discharge, often 1 to 6 weeks.
  • Outreach Floating Support: Time-limited help once people move on, including tenancy, bills, local orientation, and community integration.

How we support

We use holistic assessments covering physical and mental health, medication, daily living, education, employment, accommodation, finances, and social wellbeing.

Residents are actively involved in creating personalised support plans with regular review meetings to track progress and adapt interventions.

Our recovery model is strengths-based, focused on what people can do and how to build from that.

Our staff

Our team is available seven days a week, with round-the-clock support access. Staff are carefully recruited, trained, and continuously developed.

We work with dignity, respect, and collaboration at every stage of support delivery.
