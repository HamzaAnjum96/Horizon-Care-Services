Supported Accommodation

Horizon Care Services provides supported accommodation for people with mental health needs and learning disabilities.

The supported accommodation service provides a home with care and support tailored to individual needs.

The aim is to support residents to:
- Build independence
- Take control of their lives
- Build self-confidence
- Develop practical and daily living skills
- Progress in their recovery journey
- Achieve personal aspirations

Supported accommodation schemes include:

1. Adult Placement Scheme

The Adult Placement Scheme provides care and support for individuals who need an extended period of support during recovery.

Typical placement duration:
- 6 months to 2 years

Placement length depends on:
- Individual needs
- Level of risk
- Life goals

As individuals become ready for independent living, Horizon Care Services works with Local Authorities and NHS Trusts to support transition.

This includes:
- Housing list registrations
- Property searches
- Viewing suitable accommodation

2. Short-Term Support

Short-Term Support provides stability and intensive temporary support for individuals who need help to recover and integrate into the community.

Typical placement duration:
- 1 to 6 months

The scheme focuses on:
- Building independence
- Improving wellbeing strategies
- Building practical skills
- Increasing self-confidence
- Building resilience
- Reducing relapse risk in the community

3. Step-Down Transitional Support

Step-Down Transitional Support is for individuals ready for hospital discharge who need short-term intensive support to transition into the community.

Typical referral sources:
- NHS
- Local Authorities

Typical support duration:
- 1 to 6 weeks

The support focuses on:
- Settling after hospital discharge
- Reducing relapse risk
- Supporting housing or benefits challenges
- Working towards accommodation or community placement
- Weekly progress monitoring

4. Outreach Floating Support

Outreach Floating Support is for individuals who have recently moved from supported accommodation into independent living.

The support is time-limited and helps with community integration.

Support activities include:
- Orientation to the new area
- Tenancy management
- Utility bill organisation
- Setting up the new living space
- Connecting with local support networks
- Promoting community engagement
